History in Focus

The Mali Empire rose under Sundiata Keita and blossomed through the reign of Mansa Musa, whose pilgrimage and patronage of learning placed Timbuktu on the world map. Manuscript libraries, trade caravans, and a network of scholarship shaped a civilization whose legacy we celebrate.

Centers

Our Vision

The Musa Project uses festivals, education and community programs to reconnect people with West Africa’s intellectual, artistic, and commercial heritage — providing a platform for artisans, researchers and young leaders.

Education & Research

Scholar residencies and archival digitization partnerships highlight Timbuktu’s manuscripts and Africa’s intellectual history.

The Musa Project

Griots & Oral Memory

Griots — the living libraries — keep histories and genealogies alive, blending song, poetry and counsel.

Weaving & Textiles

Indigo dyeing, narrow-loom weaving and pattern languages form a visual vocabulary of identity and place.

Manuscripts & Learning

Preserved manuscripts tell the story of literacy, law and science — which we interpret and present through exhibitions.

Scholar's Library

Timbuktu Manuscripts

Digitization projects and curated exhibits make fragile manuscripts accessible to modern audiences.

Residencies

Scholars in residence collaborate with local historians and hosts to create public lectures and materials.

Workshops

Public workshops on paleography, conservation and Mahgreb/Sahel trade histories.

The Musa Project is more than a festival it’s a living tribute to the legacy of the Mali Empire and the golden age of West Africa. Through storytelling, traditional music, fashion, food, and architecture, we bring history to life and reconnect communities with the greatness that once defined an empire. From the epic of Sundiata to the rhythm of the drums and the spices of the royal feast, every detail is crafted to honor our roots and inspire a new generation. Join the journey. Remember the empire.
