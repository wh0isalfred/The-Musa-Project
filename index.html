Welcome to the Mali Empire Festival, a vibrant celebration that transports visitors to the golden age of Medieval West Africa. Step into a world inspired by the legendary cities of Timbuktu and Koumbi-Saleh, where music fills the air, markets bustle with merchants and artisans, and the spirit of community thrives. From the grandeur of Mansa Musa’s royal court to the everyday life of skilled metalworkers, storytellers, and farmers, every corner offers a glimpse into the beauty, ingenuity, and culture of this remarkable era. Whether you arrive in traditional attire or casual dress, the Mali Empire Festival promises an unforgettable journey through history, heritage, and the heartbeat of a thriving African civilization.

Experience the golden age to the Mali empire in a multi-day immersive festival

A dramatic retelling of the founding story of the Mali Empire experience with live performances.

Caravan Rules

  • Respect fellow travelers and festival staff.
  • Follow all safety instructions during events.
  • No littering – keep the grounds clean.
  • Respect cultural performances and traditions.
  • Festival wristbands must be worn at all times.
  • Alcohol and drugs are strictly prohibited.
  • Minors must be accompanied by a guardian.
  • Emergencies should be reported immediately.
  • Pets are not allowed.
  • All Areas are Smoke Free.
  • Amplified sound (e.g. speakers, cell phone speakers) is not permitted within the Festival.

The Musa Project is more than a festival it’s a living tribute to the legacy of the Mali Empire and the golden age of West Africa. Through storytelling, traditional music, fashion, food, and architecture, we bring history to life and reconnect communities with the greatness that once defined an empire. From the epic of Sundiata to the rhythm of the drums and the spices of the royal feast, every detail is crafted to honor our roots and inspire a new generation. Join the journey. Remember the empire. Celebrate the spirit.
